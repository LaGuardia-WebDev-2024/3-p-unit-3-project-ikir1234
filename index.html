Boston Red Sox

Record: 81-81 (.500)

  • Home: 38-43
  • Away: 43-38

Led by franchise player, Rafael Devers, the Boston Red Sox ended the season with a .500 record. Failed to reach the playoffs, but ended at third place in the AL East division. Although not their best season, the Red Sox team is filled with young talent, with players such as Jarren and Triston Casas, rookies Wilyer Abreu and Cedanne Rafaela, there is much to be expected of them in the coming years.

New York Mets

Record: 89-73 (.549)

  • Home: 46-35
  • Away: 43-38

Located in Queens, New York, the Mets started the season with a 22-33 record, and thanks to Grimace and singer Jose Iglesias, ended the season with a 89-73 record, clinching a spot in the playoffs. Led by veteran shortstop Francisco Lindor who put up MVP caliber numbers, the Mets' historic run ended in the National League Championship Series against the Los Angeles Dodger.

Los Angeles Dodgers

Record: 98-64 (.605)

  • Home: 52-29
  • Away: 46-35

After signing $700 million dollar contract to two-time MVP Shohei Ohtani, the Los Angeles Dodgers were the favorites to win the World Series, as long as they didn't choke like previous years. Although fans were skeptical of Ohtani's success this year as he battles a Tommy John surgery, Ohtani delivered with one of the most historic seasons in MLB history, being the first player of the 50-50 club; 50 Home Runs and 50 Stolen Bases. The Dodgers ended the regular season with the best record in the National League, and is now headed to the World Series.

Philadelphia Phillies

Record: 95-67 (.586)

  • Home: 54-27
  • Away: 41-40

Winning the National League East division for the second year in a row, the Philadelphia Phillies have become an unexpected playoff favorite and regular. Filled with both veteran talent, and young talent as well, the Phillies are a dominating team. Lead off hitter and veteran Kyle Schwarber has transformed the definition of a lead off hitter. Followed by two-time MVP Bryce Harper and transformative hitters Trea Turner and Alec Bohm, the Phillies have one of the most formiadble offenses in the MLB.

Detroit Tigers

Record: 86-76 (.531)

  • Home: 43-38
  • Away: 43-38

By defying all odds, the Detroit Tigers have been the most suprising team of the 2024 MLB Season. On August 11, with about a little more than a month left in the regular season, the Detroit Tigers had a 0.2% chance of making the playoffs. But the Tigers didn't give up, and proceeded to put up a historical run: since August 10, the Detroit Tigers went 31-11. And in their last game of the regular season at home against the Chicago White Sox (the worst team in MLB history), they won and clinched the third and final spot for the AL Wild Card, officially heading to the playoffs for the first time since 2014. No one on the team except Matt Vierling had ever played in the playoffs, and even Matt Vierling wasn't a regular player when he was with the Phillies. Made up of young, talented athletes, the Detroit Tigers have much more room and time to improve, even if they lost against the Clevlend Guardians in the American League Divisional Series, the have become unexpected favorites for many fans.
